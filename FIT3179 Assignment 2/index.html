GLOBAL COUNTRIES DISCOVERIES

This visualization provides a deep dive into global socio-geographic insights, encompassing data on population density, economic sectors, climate, migration, literacy, and more, offering a multifaceted view of countries from around the world. Let's dive into the intricate relationships that define our global community, charting the course to uncover unique insights, one chart at a time.

Note: Please be advised that certain charts offer the functionality to select a specific region using the dropdown region selector and additional information or insights can be accessed by hovering over the charts.

Population Density per Square Mile of Countries

The choropleth map uses green shades to display population density , with darker greens indicating higher density and lighter greens representing lower density. Notably, Asia has notably higher population density, driven by countries like India and China, where large populations are concentrated in smaller areas. In contrast, regions like North America, South America, and Africa generally exhibit lower population densities due to their larger land areas with fewer people.

Regional Economic Sector Distribution in Selected Countries with GDP

Different economic sectors, which encompass service, agriculture, and industry, are pivotal components that contribute to a country's overall economic output and structure. With this chart, you can compare the economic structures of countries in a selected region and understand the significance of these sectors in their economies.

Net Migration by Country in Different World Regions

Net migration quantifies the balance of people entering and leaving a country over a specific period. In this context, Net Migration value of 0 means equal inflow and outflow, resulting in a population equilibrium. A negative Net Migration value suggests that more people are leaving the country than entering, leading to a population decrease. A positive Net Migration value indicates that more people are entering the country than leaving, resulting in a population increase.

Relationship between Literacy Rate and Phone Usage in Countries

Each circle represents a country, color-coded by region. Notably, there is no clear positive correlation between these two variables. Countries with lower literacy rates tend to have lower phone usage, however, other countries with higher literacy rates exhibit varying phone usage. This suggests that the presence of phone usage does not necessarily correlate with higher literacy rates and vice versa.

Relationship between Climate and Crop Composition in Countries

Climate 1 corresponds to dry tropical or tundra and ice, Climate 2 to wet tropical, Climate 3 to temperate humid subtropical and temperate continental, and Climate 4 to Dry hot summers and wet winters. Notably, Climate 4 has the fewest countries and lower crop production, while Climate 2 stands out as the climate type is associative with more countries and higher crop production.
